IT Project Idea

My IT project idea is in the health sector. As a primary carer for my mother who is both a heart attack and stroke victim, as well as being diabetic, this idea was inspired by her medication needs. The idea can be implemented as possibly an app, or a chatbot. It would essentially work as a digital Webster pack, with the idea of reminders and schedules. With the chatbot, each time a medication has been taken, it can be logged or ticked off. In addition to this, the chatbot would be set up in such a way that if a medication for the day and time has not been logged, it can send a message to the user to remind them they have missed their medication. Often, I would notice my mother skip her medication either by forgetting, or even after it had been taken, she would forget if she had taken it. Although physical Webster packs already exist, they need to be prepared each week. This chatbot can have a set schedule, with only needing to be prepared for each individual one time. It could also serve another purpose that it would contain a log to help the user fill physical Webster packs each week. As stroke is the third leading cause of death in Australia, there is the possibility of widespread use across the country, as many stroke victims are disabled after the event. This product could be designed as a chatbot, but also possibly as a app as well. Initially, once a user has been seen by a Doctor and prescribed medication, the user will input the data into the chatbot or app. This data would include the exact name of the medication, what days it should be taken and at what time, the correct dosage, and whether it should be taken with food or not. In addition to this, the user could input and record any other relevant information such as having a bad reaction to medication, unusual side effects, whether it has been effective or not, as well as any allergies. A warning could be implemented if any future prescriptions could cause allergic reactions that may have been overlooked during medical consultation. As the goal of a Webster-pak is to aide in adherence to medication (WebsterCare 2020), the use of a chatbot or app in this sense could further support that goal. While Webster-pak’s alone do help with this, the ability to receive reminders, record data and use it for medical recording via a device such as a phone or tablet could help a lot as these devices have become so widespread and are used by many people multiple times throughout the day. This repeated exposure to digital devices could very well increase the likelihood that users are reminded to take their medication, what dosage, and at what time. For development, a Chatbot can be created using a coded and non-coded solution. The non-coded solution such as Chatfuel can be an easier approach, but it also has limitations. (Goyal, A 2020). The coded solution can be customised far more. There is Microsoft Bot Framework, wit.ai by Facebook, and Google’s Dialogflow. Devices for testing would also be needed. On the user end, it would be designed primarily for devices such as smart phones and tablets, as these are more widespread and readily accessible for users. Use on PC would be possible, but smart devices offer more accessibility, especially for the disabled as their movements may be limited. The skills required for this should not be too high of a requirement, but still not an easy task for an early programmer. It requires that I become proficient in the development software previously stated, specifically the code-based solutions. Although a non-code-based solution is possible, it is likely that it may not meet the requirements for my goal. Hardware should be relatively easy as the programming can be written on a PC, then tested on a device or possibly a virtual environment. The real difficulty as previously stated, lies in learning the programming language for the chosen development software. If this project is successful, the projected outcome is that users will have a higher level of adherence to their required medications. As many who suffer from a stroke, heart attack or other conditions could see these conditions become worse if the correct medications are not taken regularly, the use of this chatbot could help reduce future risks to the user, attributing to overall better health and possibly even a higher quality of life. The one who benefits most from this outcome is the user ultimately.
